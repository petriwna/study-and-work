Career Opportunities After Graduation

International students in Canada can gain valuable work experience after completing their studies. Here's how:

  • Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)

    Graduates from eligible designated learning institutions (DLIs) can apply for a Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP). This open work permit allows you to work in Canada for up to 3 years depending on the length of your study program.

  • Permanent Residency Pathways

    • Canadian Experience Class (CEC):

      After gaining Canadian work experience through PGWP, you may be eligible to apply for PR under the Canadian Experience Class via Express Entry.

    • Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs):

      Provinces like British Columbia, Ontario, and Quebec offer PR options for international graduates with job offers in in-demand occupations. Visit the official provincial immigration portals for more details.

  • In-Demand Occupations

    Canada actively seeks skilled professionals in areas such as healthcare, engineering, IT, education, and skilled trades. Your education in Canada may align with these sectors, increasing your chances for employment and immigration success.

    Learn more about work permits and immigration through the official IRCC website.
